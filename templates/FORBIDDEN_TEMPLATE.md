## Forbidden phrases

- "game-changer"
- "must-have"
- "you won't believe"
- "in this post"
- "in this article"
- "in this guide"
- "this guide covers"
- "this post will"
- "without further ado"
- "let's dive in"
- "let's get started"
- "at the end of the day"
- "it goes without saying"
- "in today's world"
- "in this day and age"
- "when it comes to"
- "it's no secret that"
- "the fact of the matter is"
- "needless to say"
- "last but not least"


## Forbidden constructions

- Rhetorical questions as openers (unless the question is the reader's actual
  query and the answer immediately follows)
- "Whether you're a beginner or an expert" or any variation of the
  "regardless of your skill level" construction
- "Have you ever wondered" or similar false-curiosity openers
- "As we all know" or "Everyone knows that" -- either cite it or don't
  claim consensus


## Forbidden words (context-dependent)

<!-- These words are not always forbidden but should never be used in the
     ways described: -->

- "literally" (when used as emphasis, not literally)
- "incredibly" / "amazingly" / "absolutely" (empty intensifiers)
- "revolutionary" / "groundbreaking" (unless describing an actual revolution
  or actual new ground)
