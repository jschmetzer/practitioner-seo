# Formatting Rules -- benchcraftworkshop.com


## Trademark and brand formatting

- **Lie-Nielsen** -- always hyphenated, both words capitalized. Never
  "Lie Nielsen" or "lie-nielsen."
- **Veritas** -- capitalized. Made by Lee Valley Tools; reference as
  "Veritas (Lee Valley)" on first mention if context requires the parent
  company, "Veritas" thereafter.
- **Stanley** -- capitalized. When referencing vintage planes, include the
  era: "vintage Stanley No. 4" or "Stanley Bailey No. 4."
- **Narex** -- capitalized.
- **Shapton** -- capitalized.
- **Norton** -- capitalized. For sharpening stones specifically, include the
  line: "Norton India" or "Norton Crystolon."
- **DMT** -- always uppercase. Stands for Diamond Machining Technology.
  "DMT DiaSharp" on first reference, "DMT" thereafter.

## Tool naming conventions

- Hand plane models: "No. 4" with capital N, period after "No", space before
  the number. Never "#4" or "number 4" or "no.4."
- Plane types by function: "smoothing plane", "jack plane", "jointer plane",
  "block plane", "shoulder plane" -- lowercase unless starting a sentence.
- When specifying manufacturer + model: "Lie-Nielsen No. 4 smoothing plane"
  -- manufacturer first, model number, then functional type.
- Chisel widths: expressed in fractions -- "1/4-inch chisel", "3/4-inch
  chisel." Hyphenate when used as a compound adjective before a noun.


## Measurement standards

- **Primary system:** Imperial (inches, feet, pounds). This is a North
  American woodworking audience.
- **Blade and shaving measurements:** Decimal inches for precision settings.
  "Set the blade to take a 0.002-inch shaving." Not "two thousandths."
- **Board dimensions:** Fractions. "3/4-inch thick", "6 inches wide",
  "36 inches long." The inch symbol (") is acceptable in tables but
  spell out "inches" in body copy.
- **Angles:** Degrees with the degree symbol in tables, spelled out in body
  copy. "Sharpen the primary bevel at 25 degrees" in body text; "25deg" in
  a table column is acceptable.
- **Wood dimensions (lumber):** Use nominal and actual where the distinction
  matters. "A nominal 1x6 (actual 3/4 x 5-1/2 inches)."
- **Metric:** Include metric equivalents in parentheses only when the tool
  or product is sold in metric specifications. "Shapton 1000-grit stone
  (210 x 70 x 15mm)."


## Wood species naming

- Common name on first reference, followed by botanical name in parentheses
  if the species is uncommon or the distinction matters for the technique:
  "white oak (Quercus alba)."
- Common names are lowercase unless they begin a sentence: "cherry",
  "walnut", "white oak", "hard maple."
- Do not italicize common names. Do italicize botanical names: "*Quercus alba*."
- When comparing species, use the common name consistently throughout.
  Don't switch between "cherry" and "Prunus serotina" in the same section.


## Grit progression notation

- Express as a sequence separated by forward slashes:
  "220 / 1000 / 4000 / 8000 grit"
- "Grit" is lowercase and appears after the final number only, not after each.
- When referencing a specific stone at a specific grit: "Shapton Pro 1000"
  (manufacturer + line + grit number, no "grit" suffix needed).


## Book title formatting

- Sarah's books: *The Unplugged Workshop* and *Joinery by Hand* -- always
  italicized, full title, no abbreviations.
- Other woodworking books: italicized on first reference with author name.
  "Chris Schwarz's *The Anarchist's Tool Chest*." Subsequent references
  can use title only in italics.


## Numbers

- Spell out one through nine in body copy. Numerals for 10 and above.
- Exception: all measurements use numerals regardless of size. "A 2-inch
  blade" not "a two-inch blade."
- Exception: model numbers always use numerals. "No. 4" not "No. four."
- Currency: "$45" with no decimal for whole dollar amounts. "$45.99" with
  decimal when cents matter. Comma separator for thousands: "$1,200."


## Hyphenation

- Compound adjectives before a noun are hyphenated: "hand-cut dovetails",
  "25-degree bevel", "3/4-inch stock."
- Compound adjectives after a noun are not: "the dovetails were hand cut",
  "the bevel is 25 degrees."
- "Hand tool" is two words (noun phrase), not hyphenated. "Hand-tool
  woodworking" is hyphenated (compound adjective).
